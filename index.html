Correlations Discovered Between Smoking Percentage and Age, Income

In this Data Driven Document (D3), We explore certain associations between demographics and health risk factors using survey data collected from the American Community Survey (ACS) and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) during the year 2014. Each marker represents an individual state in the US. Hover over any state within the scatter plot to start exploring!

We examined the outcome (for state-level data) of smoking percentages based on two factors, median age, and median household income. We found a positive correlation between smoking percentages and median age of individuals. Conversely, we see negative correlations between smoking percentages and median household income.
